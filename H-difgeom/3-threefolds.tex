\sct{Thurston geometries}
Ref: \cite{MF2010,Taubes2015}.

\ssc{Dimension $3$}

Ref: Geometrization conjecture~\cite{Thurston1982}, Ricci flow~\cite{Hamilton1982}, Entropy formula~\cite{Perelman2002}.


There are only eight different pairs $(X,G)$ where $X$ is a smooth $3$-dimensional simply connected manifold with a transitive action of Lie group $G$ with compact stabilizers and $G$ is maximal(\cite[Section~4]{Thurston1982}):

\begin{enr}
	\item Spherical geometry. $X=S^{3}$ and $G=\OO(4)$.
	\item Euclidean geometry. $X=E^{3}$ and $G=\bR^{3}\tms\OO(3)$.
	\item Hyperbolic geometry. $X=\bH^{3}$ and $G=\OO(1,3)$
	\item $X=S^{2}\tms E^{1}$
	\item $X=\bH^{2}\tms E^{1}$
	\item $X=\oT{\SL}(2)$
	\item Nilmanifold
	\item Solvmanifold
\end{enr}
